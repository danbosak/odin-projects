Tacos

Tacos are a staple of Mexican cuisine consisting of a tortilla (corn or flour) folded or rolled around a variety of fillings. Common fillings include seasoned ground beef, chicken, pork, or beans, often topped with fresh ingredients like lettuce, tomatoes, cheese, and sour cream. Tacos are versatile and can be customized with different sauces, salsas, and garnishes to suit personal taste.

Ingredients

  • Tortillas
  • Beef
  • Vegetables

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Meat Filling:
    1. Heat a skillet over medium heat.
    2. Add ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon.
    3. Drain excess fat if necessary.
    4. Add chopped onion and garlic, and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
    5. Stir in taco seasoning and water. Simmer for 5-10 minutes until thickened.
  2. Prepare the Tacos:
    1. Warm the taco shells or tortillas according to package instructions.
    2. Fill each taco shell with the meat mixture.
    3. Top with shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, shredded cheddar cheese, sour cream, and chopped cilantro.
    4. Serve with lime wedges on the side.
