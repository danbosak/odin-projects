Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish consisting of layers of wide, flat pasta sheets interspersed with rich fillings.

Typically, it includes a combination of meat sauce (like Bolognese), béchamel or ricotta cheese, and mozzarella. The dish is baked until bubbly and golden. Lasagna is known for its hearty, comforting flavors and is often enjoyed as a main course at family gatherings and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • Pasta sheets
  • Tomato sauce
  • Cheese

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Meat Sauce:
    1. Heat a large skillet or saucepan over medium heat.
    2. Add ground beef (and Italian sausage if using) and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon.
    3. Drain excess fat if necessary.
    4. Add chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
    5. Stir in minced garlic and cook for 1 minute.
    6. Add crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, dried basil, dried oregano, salt, and pepper.
    7. Bring to a simmer and cook for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  2. Prepare the Ricotta Mixture:
    1. In a bowl, combine ricotta cheese, egg, parsley, and grated Parmesan cheese.
    2. Mix well and set aside.
  3. Cook the Lasagna Noodles:
    1. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions until al dente.
    2. Drain and rinse with cold water to prevent sticking.
  4. Preheat the Oven:
    1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  5. Assemble the Lasagna:
    1. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish.
    2. Place a layer of lasagna noodles over the sauce.
    3. Spread a portion of the ricotta mixture over the noodles.
    4. Sprinkle with a layer of shredded mozzarella cheese.
    5. Repeat the layers (meat sauce, noodles, ricotta mixture, mozzarella) until all ingredients are used, ending with a layer of meat sauce.
    6. Sprinkle additional shredded mozzarella and Parmesan cheese on top.
  6. Bake the Lasagna:
    1. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil.
    2. Bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes.
    3. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the top is bubbly and golden brown.
  7. Cool and Serve:
    1. Allow the lasagna to cool for about 15 minutes before serving. This helps the layers set and makes it easier to cut.
